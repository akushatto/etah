The Living History of Etah

Ancient

Aintha — The Origin

The city was originally called Aintha, named by the ruler of Awagarh. One legend says a traveller searching for water struck a brick (eint) underground, giving rise to the name "Einta," which evolved into Etah.

Medieval

Mughal Era & Amir Khusro

Patiyali, a town in the Etah district, is celebrated as the birthplace of the legendary poet and musician Amir Khusro — one of the greatest voices of Urdu and Persian literature. The Mughal influence shaped Etah's architecture, including the Jama Masjid.

1857

Sepoy Mutiny — A Glorious Uprising

Etah stood as a major base of the legendary 1857 Sepoy Mutiny against British rule, making it an important landmark in India's first war of independence.

Founded

Established by Dil Sukh Rai Bahadur

The city of Etah was formally established during the reign of Dil Sukh Rai Bahadur, whose legacy lives on through the grand Kailash Mandir — a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, built over 148 years ago.

Places to Discover

Landmarks of Etah

🕌
Awagarh Fort

A magnificent historical fortification built by the King of Awagarh, located about 24 km from Etah city. The fort stands as a proud relic of the Awagarh princely state and draws history enthusiasts from across the region.

🗺 24 km from City
🛕
Kailash Mandir

An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, built over 148 years ago by king Dil Sukh Rai Bahadur. Known for its stunning carvings, intricate artwork, and serene spiritual atmosphere that captivates devotees and tourists alike.

🙏 Religious Site
🕌
Jama Masjid

A magnificent mosque in the heart of Etah city, attributed to the Mughal era. Known for its intricate carvings, beautiful domes, and fine example of classical Mughal architecture. Most vibrant during Eid celebrations.

🏛 Mughal Heritage
🦅
Patna Pakshi Vihar

A bird sanctuary within 10 km of Etah, designated by the Government of UP in 1990. A heavenly haven for nearly 200 species of exotic birds, particularly stunning in winter months after the monsoon fills its lake.

🌿 Nature Sanctuary
🌊
Soron — Sukar Kshetra

A sacred Hindu pilgrimage centre along the banks of the Ganges, to the north of Etah. The riverbank of Ganga in Soron, known as Kachhala, is famous for the Shooker Mahotsava festival drawing thousands of devotees each year.

🙏 Sacred Pilgrimage
🏫
Gurukul Vidyalaya & Yagyashala

Home to one of the world's second largest Yagyashalas — a sacred fire-ritual complex. This ancient seat of learning preserves Vedic traditions and is a landmark of spiritual education in the region.

📚 Spiritual Education
🕰️
Ghanta Ghar

The iconic Clock Tower of Etah, a popular gathering landmark in the city center. Along with Hathi Gate and Thandi Sadak, it forms the heart of old Etah's urban heritage and social life.

🏙 City Icon
🛕
Kali Temple

Over 250 years old, this is one of the oldest temples in Etah, dedicated to Goddess Kali. Located on Jalesar Road, it is renowned for its ancient architecture and the powerful daily prayers and rituals performed within.

🛕 Ancient Temple
🌳
Mehta Park & Padav Maidan

Mehta Park is beloved for its famous tangy samosas and relaxing greenery. Padav Maidan serves as the grand stage for Dussehra, Ram Lila, and city-wide festivals, exhibitions, and cultural events throughout the year.

🌿 Leisure & Festivals
Traditions & Flavours

Culture of Etah

🎭
Fairs & Festivals

The city comes alive during Ram Lila, Dussehra, Shooker Mahotsava at the Ganga ghats of Soron, and Mahaveer Jayanti. These celebrations reflect the deep Hindu and syncretic traditions that have shaped Etah's cultural identity over centuries.

🍛
Cuisine

Etah's food is a blend of Mughlai richness and UP rural warmth. Dum biryani, mutton korma, seekh kababs, and the famed local samosas of Mehta Park are culinary treasures. Spiced with Agra's heritage, every bite tells a story.

🎵
Amir Khusro's Legacy

Born in Patiyali, Etah, Amir Khusro is considered among the greatest poets of Urdu and Persian — inventor of the sitar, pioneer of qawwali, and the voice that bridged cultures. Etah is the sacred birthplace of this immortal artiste.

🌾
Agriculture & Doab Fertility

Etah sits on the fertile Ganga-Yamuna Doab, irrigated by the Upper and Lower Ganges canals. Wheat, cotton, sugarcane, bajra, jowar, barley, and maize thrive here. Agriculture remains the beating heart of the district's economy and identity.

🏺
Crafts & Jalesar Brass

The town of Jalesar in the Etah district is renowned across India for its exquisite brass and bell-metal handicrafts. These traditional artisans have passed down their craft across generations, creating beautiful idols, utensils, and decorative works.

📖
Education & Learning

Etah hosts several colleges affiliated with Dr. B.R. Ambedkar University, Agra. The Gurukul Vidyalaya continues Vedic educational traditions while modern institutions keep the district connected to contemporary learning and progress.

Location & Landscape

Where Etah Stands

Etah lies in western Uttar Pradesh, positioned strategically midway on the Delhi–Kanpur highway. Situated about 40 km southwest of the Ganges River and 72 km northeast of Agra, it occupies the fertile alluvial plains of the Ganga-Yamuna Doab. The district is surrounded by Firozabad, Aligarh, Kasganj, Badaun, and Farrukhabad.

The Yamuna Expressway runs close by, placing Etah within a comfortable 3-hour drive (207 km) from New Delhi. The region is also well-linked to Agra, Vrindavan, and Mathura via NH-91 and the Grand Trunk Road.
